## What went well

- **Single audited stock gateway** (`lib/stock.ts`): every quantity change (purchase, usage, stocktake, restaurant serving) flows through one function that writes the immutable movement row and triggers low-stock — on-hand can never drift from its history.
- **One escalation helper** (`lib/escalate.ts`): `raiseEscalation` reused by failed payments, missing/damaged assets, low stock, and the hourly SLA sweep, with open-dedup. One concept, one chokepoint.
- **Money reuse**: Epic 9 charge-to-room posts through the existing Epic 5 ledger engine — no new money paths, which is why there were zero incidents.
- **Honest notification engine**: unconfigured channels stay `queued` (not fake `sent`); SMS flips to `sent` only on a real gateway 200; 3-attempt retry then `failed`.
- **Pattern reuse throughout**: `requirePermission` gating, int64 cents, audit logs, codegen→typecheck→test→build pipeline. Four epics, no new architectural pattern.

## What was bumpy (systemic — no blame)

- **Stale Convex codegen broke the web typecheck repeatedly** — adding a backend function failed `apps/web` `tsc` until `convex codegen` was rerun; rediscovered several times.
- **Vercel deploy failure** — a stray `apps/web/.vercel` link pointed at project `web` instead of `fammycomforts`, so `npm install` ran in the wrong context and failed. Deploy config has been the recurring sharp edge across the project.
- **Test-authoring gotchas** — Vitest `toMatchObject` treats explicit `undefined` strictly (switched to `toBeUndefined()`); one test referenced `upsertTaxRule` when the real export is `createTaxRule` (assumed instead of verified).
- **Sprint tracker drift** — `sprint-status.yaml` showed Epics 7–10 as `backlog` while they were built/tested/deployed; corrected this session. Root cause: definition-of-done didn't include updating the tracker.
