## Epic Quality Review

### Best-Practices Compliance
- ✅ **FR traceability:** every story tags its FRs.
- ✅ **Starter rule:** Epic 1 Story 1.1 is the Turborepo scaffold (matches Architecture AR1).
- ✅ **Just-in-time tables:** no "create all tables" story; each story owns only its entities.
- ✅ **Within-epic ordering:** stories build on prior stories; no backward-breaking sequences found.

### 🔴 Critical Violations
- None that block *starting* (Epics 1–3 are clean and independent).

### 🟠 Major Issues
1. **Cross-epic forward dependency: Payments (Epic 6) is sequenced after Guest Booking (4) and Front Desk (5), which need it.**
   - Story 4.6 records payment *intent* (mitigated), but **Story 4.9 (guest portal invoices/receipts)** and **Story 5.5 (checkout balance/receipt)** genuinely depend on the Epic 6 money engine/ledger.
   - **Impact:** building Epics 4–5 fully before Epic 6 leaves payment recording/receipts stubbed.
   - **Recommendation:** pull the **money engine + ledger (Story 6.2)** and basic payment recording (6.4) forward to sit *before/with* Epic 5, or explicitly defer Stories 4.9 and the receipt half of 5.5 until Epic 6 lands. Decide this at sprint planning.
2. **Oversized stories (not single-dev-session sized):**
   - **1.3** (entire component library), **1.4** (full PWA: SW + manifest + offline + Lighthouse), **5.5** (checkout: balance + asset check + receipt + housekeeping trigger), **6.3** (M-Pesa STK end-to-end incl. webhook).
   - **Recommendation:** split each into 2–3 stories during sprint planning / `create-story`.
3. **Three open specs are referenced by ACs but not yet defined** (carried from Architecture gap analysis):
   - Field-level **Prisma schema** (needed before Epic 3/4 data stories).
   - **Offline conflict-resolution policy** (Story 7.5 — R2, so lower urgency).
   - **M-Pesa Daraja integration spec** (Story 6.3 — R1, **urgent for MVP**).

### 🟡 Minor Concerns
